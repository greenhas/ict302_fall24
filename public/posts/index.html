What is Content Aggregation? In Web Content Management, author Deane Barker defines content aggregation as “an intentional grouping of content for purposes of organization or presentation” (Barker, 2016). In other words, this means that content aggregation is the way that content is displayed on a website in order for users to view said content via either the organization of the website or the terms that the user chooses to see. Content aggregation is simply the way that the content is displayed with other content on websites.
